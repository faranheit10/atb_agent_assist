SECTION A: ATB FINANCIAL COMPANY PROFILE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Full name: ATB Financial (Alberta Treasury Branches)
Founded: 1938 as Alberta Treasury Branches
Ownership: Crown corporation, owned by the Province of Alberta
Headquarters: 10020 100th Street NW, Edmonton, Alberta
Operates in: Alberta only (including national parks: Banff, Jasper, Waterton)

Key facts:
  - Largest financial institution based in Alberta
  - Largest public bank in North America
  - Over 800,000 personal and business clients served
  - Over 170 branches across Alberta
  - Major cities: 24 branches in Calgary, 32 branches in Edmonton, 2 in Red Deer
  - Over 5,000 employees
  - Total assets: Over $55 billion
  - Deposits guaranteed fully by the Government of Alberta (NOT CDIC member)
  - Not a federally chartered bank; regulated entirely by Government of Alberta under ATB Financial Act

Deposit guarantee: All ATB deposits (chequing, savings, GICs, registered accounts) are 100% guaranteed by the Province of Alberta. This is comparable to CDIC protection offered by chartered banks, but applies only to ATB accounts.

Primary service lines:
  - Personal Banking (chequing, savings, investing, borrowing, mortgages, credit cards)
  - Business Banking (business accounts, commercial lending, agriculture)
  - Wealth Management (investment advice, Compass Portfolios, private banking)
  - Mortgage Lending
  - Insurance

Recent milestone (2025): ATB Financial acquired Cormark Securities Inc. to expand investment banking activities.

ATB Personal is ATB's primary digital banking platform (web and mobile app).

Key features available in ATB Personal:
  - Account balances and 180 days of transaction history
  - Bill payments (schedule or instant)
  - Interac e-Transfer® (send and receive)
  - Global Transfers (international wire transfers, $0–$15 depending on amount and destination)
  - Transfer between ATB accounts
  - Mobile cheque deposit (via phone camera)
  - Loan and mortgage payment processing
  - Credit card management: activate, lock/unlock, change PIN, view statements
  - ATB TrackIt: budgeting, spending categories, goal tracking, debt repayment planner
  - CRA direct deposit enrollment
  - Open new accounts
  - Add to investments
  - View RRSP, TFSA, and investment account details
  - E-statement enrollment (recommended to avoid paper statement fees)
  - Credit score check (for existing borrowing clients)

Two-factor authentication (2FA): MANDATORY for all ATB Personal users (as of recent policy update)
  - If client cannot complete 2FA: call 1-800-332-8383 to reset
  - If client cannot log in: start by resetting password/username; if unresolved, Client Care can assist

ATB Personal app changes (May 2021): ATB Mobile app was retired; all clients now use ATB Personal app

IMPORTANT: In-branch transactions that are not routine (bank drafts, USD in-branch transactions, teller-assisted) may incur fees depending on account type (e.g., $5 fee on No-Fee All-In Digital Account for in-branch transactions).

SECTION D: DIGITAL TRANSFERS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

INTERAC e-TRANSFER®:
  - Cost: $1.50 each to send (many chequing accounts include a limited or unlimited number of e-Transfers at no fee)
  - Free to receive
  - How: Via ATB Personal — only need recipient's email address
  - Speed: Minutes for most recipients

GLOBAL TRANSFERS (International):
  - Cost: $0–$15 depending on amount and destination country
  - How: Available through ATB Personal
  - Conversion: Foreign exchange rate applied on the day the transaction is processed
  - Up to 2.5% above Mastercard rate applies for some currency conversions

CANADIAN BANK-TO-BANK TRANSFERS:
  - Transfer to any Canadian bank account using institution code, transit number, and account number
  - Available through ATB Personal

ABM (ATM) NETWORK:
  - ATB ABMs: Free within Alberta (for most accounts)
  - Non-ATB ABMs (within Alberta): Regular fees apply; client notified before proceeding
  - Non-ATB ABMs (outside Alberta, within Canada): ATB reimburses a set amount per account type (e.g., up to $4.50/withdrawal for Unlimited Account — 2 per month; up to $4.50 for ATB Advantage — 5 per month)
  - ABMs outside Canada: $5.00 per withdrawal (Cirrus® network), plus any local ABM fees; US withdrawals reimbursed $8 once per month (Unlimited Account)
  - Cirrus network abroad: Currency converted at 2.5% above Mastercard exchange rate

VISA DEBIT:
  - All ATB debit cards with Visa Debit logo work online, in-store worldwide, and for recurring payments
  - Visa Secure authentication for online fraud protection
  - Can be added to Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay

SECTION E: FRAUD & SECURITY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ATB ONLINE BANKING GUARANTEE:
  In the unlikely event of unauthorized access to the client's account, ATB will restore any funds lost due to unauthorized use — provided the client followed ATB's safe banking practices.

Safe banking practices clients must follow:
  - Never share username, password, or 2FA codes with anyone (including people claiming to be ATB)
  - Always clear browser cache after using ATB Personal on a shared computer
  - Report any suspected unauthorized access immediately to 1-800-332-8383

If a client believes their card or account has been compromised:
  1. Lock the card immediately through ATB Personal app (lock/unlock toggle)
  2. Call 1-800-332-8383 immediately (24/7)
  3. ATB will investigate and reverse unauthorized transactions per Online Banking Guarantee

Location-based fraud detection: ATB uses GPS and device data to flag unusual login locations (e.g., if client normally logs in from Lethbridge and suddenly logs in from Chicago, ATB may challenge identity)

ATB does NOT sell, share, or trade client personal information.
ATB does NOT permit third-party use of client information.
ATB does NOT ask for more personal information than needed to serve the client.

SECTION F: CUSTOMER SERVICE ESCALATION PROCEDURES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TIER 1 — AGENT HANDLES DIRECTLY:
  - Account balance inquiries
  - Transaction history questions
  - Card lock/unlock requests
  - e-Transfer issues (standard delays or failures)
  - Password/username resets (direct to digital process; escalate if unresolved)
  - Fee inquiries (explain fee schedule; waive only if clear error by ATB)
  - General product information (interest rates, account types, eligibility)
  - Basic account maintenance (update contact info, e-statement enrollment)

TIER 2 — WARM TRANSFER TO SPECIALIST:
  - Mortgage applications, renewals, and rate negotiations → Mortgage Specialist
  - Investment advice (RRSP room, portfolio selection, GIC laddering) → Investment Advisor
  - Business banking needs → Business Banking Advisor
  - Overdraft protection applications → Everyday Banking Advisor
  - Complex loan applications (secured loans, debt consolidation over $50K) → Loan Specialist
  - Private banking inquiries (clients with significant wealth/complex needs) → Private Banking Advisor
  - Credit card disputes and chargebacks → Client Care specialist team (escalate within Client Care)

TRIGGER PHRASES FOR ESCALATION:
  - "I want to speak to a manager" → Acknowledge, document reason, transfer to Team Lead or Senior Agent
  - "I want to close my account" → Retention attempt by current agent first; understand reason, offer solutions; escalate if client insists
  - "I'm going to switch to another bank" → Retention conversation; understand pain point; involve specialist if rate or product issue
  - "There was an unauthorized transaction on my account" → IMMEDIATE escalation to fraud team; advise client not to use card; file dispute
  - "Someone called me claiming to be ATB asking for my PIN" → IMMEDIATE fraud alert; advise client not to provide any info; escalate to security team
  - "I can't access my account" → Standard digital troubleshooting first; escalate to Technical Support if unresolved

TIER 3 — FORMAL COMPLAINT PROCESS:
  - When client is not satisfied after Tier 1 and Tier 2
  - Document complaint in detail
  - Provide client with ATB's formal complaint process information
  - Client may also contact the ATB Ombudsman (internal) or OBSI (Ombudsman for Banking Services and Investments) if unresolved

AGENT NOTES BEST PRACTICES:
  - Always note the reason for the call and the resolution provided
  - If client is calling about a recurring issue, flag account for supervisor review
  - If fee waiver was applied, note the reason (e.g., "system error," "first-time occurrence")
  - Never promise outcomes you cannot guarantee (e.g., "your loan will definitely be approved")

SECTION I: NEWCOMERS TO CANADA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ATB has solutions specifically for newcomers to Alberta:
  - Fee waivers and special account packages for clients who landed in Canada in the last 5 years and are Alberta residents
  - USD chequing access available
  - Students First LOC available for international students (with qualified Alberta-resident co-borrower)
  - Credit Builder LOC to establish Canadian credit history ($10,000 limit, no credit check)
  - Dedicated newcomer page at atb.com

Age-based accounts summary:
  - Under 13: Joint account with parent/guardian/grandparent only
  - 13–17: Individual account or joint with parent/guardian/grandparent; valid ID required
  - 18–25: Generation Account (no monthly fee)
  - 26+: Standard account lineup
  - 59+: Freedom Account (no monthly fee, unlimited transactions)
