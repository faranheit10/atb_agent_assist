OVERVIEW: LOANS vs LINES OF CREDIT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

LOAN:
  - Fixed lump-sum amount borrowed once
  - Fixed repayment schedule (weekly, bi-weekly, semi-monthly, or monthly)
  - Fixed end date — once repaid, loan is closed (cannot re-borrow)
  - Better for: One-time purchases (car, renovation, debt consolidation)
  - Payments include principal + interest; monthly amount is fixed

LINE OF CREDIT (LOC):
  - Revolving — borrow, repay, and borrow again up to approved limit
  - No need to reapply once approved
  - Minimum monthly payment: interest only on outstanding balance
  - Balance can be repaid in full or partially at any time — no penalty
  - Better for: Ongoing financial buffer, unpredictable expenses, flexibility

KEY DIFFERENCE vs CREDIT CARD:
  - LOC interest rates are typically lower than credit cards (19.90%+) but higher than secured loans
  - Credit card minimum payment includes interest + portion of balance; LOC minimum is interest-only
  - Both are revolving; LOC payments may vary month to month as the balance changes

LINES OF CREDIT — TYPES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

───────────────────────────────────────
1. PERSONAL LINE OF CREDIT (Unsecured)
───────────────────────────────────────
- No collateral required
- Higher interest rate than secured LOC (but lower than credit card)
- Rate: Variable, based on ATB prime rate (confirm current rate at atb.com/resources/rates)
- Apply online or call 1-800-332-8383
- Amount approved based on: credit score, income, debt-to-income ratio (ATB prefers below 42%)
- Can be used for: Anything — debt consolidation, travel, renovations, vehicle repairs, emergency

───────────────────────────────────────
2. HOME EQUITY LINE OF CREDIT (HELOC) — Secured
───────────────────────────────────────
(See Mortgage document for full details)
- Secured by home equity; lower rate than unsecured LOC
- Up to 65% of home value; up to 80% combined with mortgage
- Rate: Variable, tied to ATB prime rate
- Interest-only monthly payments

───────────────────────────────────────
3. STUDENTS FIRST LINE OF CREDIT
───────────────────────────────────────
- For post-secondary students (full-time programs, not-for-profit Canadian or US universities, minimum 12 weeks, 20+ instruction hours/week)
- Borrow up to $60,000 (or $15,000 per academic year)
- Preferred (low) interest rate during school and grace period
- 12-month grace period after graduation before repayment begins
- International students: must have a qualified Alberta-resident co-borrower
- Programs that do NOT qualify: personal development, hobby programs, programs < 12 weeks, private career programs < 20 hrs/week

───────────────────────────────────────
4. PROFESSIONAL STUDENTS LINE OF CREDIT
───────────────────────────────────────
- For students in professional programs (law, medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, etc.)
- Up to $350,000 in total financing
- Preferred interest rate
- Flexible repayment: up to 25-year repayment term after conversion to loan
- Upon graduation (after grace period): converts to personal loan at same rate
  - Up to $25,000 ($50,000 for medical/dentistry) allocated as ongoing Personal LOC
- Eligibility: Full-time program at not-for-profit Canadian or US university; credit check required (good or unestablished credit accepted)
- Proof of enrollment required annually (September)

───────────────────────────────────────
5. CREDIT BUILDER LINE OF CREDIT
───────────────────────────────────────
- $10,000 credit limit
- No credit history required
- Designed for newcomers to credit or those rebuilding
- Access funds, pay interest on amount used
- Helps establish credit score over time

PERSONAL LOANS — TYPES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

───────────────────────────────────────
STANDARD PERSONAL LOAN (Secured or Unsecured)
───────────────────────────────────────
Use cases: Car purchase or repairs, home improvements, paying off high-interest debt, travel, starting a business, education

Secured loan:
  - Collateral required (home, car, RV, boat, ATV, investments, manufactured home, raw land)
  - Lower interest rate due to reduced lender risk
  - Higher borrowing limits possible

Unsecured loan:
  - No collateral needed
  - Higher interest rate than secured
  - Typically smaller amounts, shorter repayment terms

Repayment flexibility:
  - Weekly, bi-weekly, semi-monthly, or monthly payment options
  - Skip-a-payment: Clients can skip up to the equivalent of one monthly payment per calendar year without penalty (conditions may apply)

Factors affecting rate: Credit score, income, existing debt, loan type (secured vs unsecured), loan term

───────────────────────────────────────
DEBT CONSOLIDATION LOAN
───────────────────────────────────────
Purpose: Combine multiple high-interest debts (credit cards, other loans) into one single loan at a lower interest rate
Benefits: Lower overall interest paid; single monthly payment; simplified debt management
How to use: ATB pays off the identified debts; client repays ATB at consolidated lower rate
Best for: Clients with 2+ high-interest debts who want to streamline payments

───────────────────────────────────────
RRSP LOAN
───────────────────────────────────────
Purpose: Allows clients to maximize RRSP contributions (and tax deduction) even if they don't have the cash available
Two types:
  1. Current-year RRSP loan: Borrow to contribute to this year's RRSP
  2. Catch-up RRSP loan: Borrow to contribute unused RRSP room from previous years (up to $75,000)
Interest: Flexible interest rates (confirm with ATB advisor)
Repayment: Typically repaid within 1 year (to minimize interest)
Tax refund strategy: Use tax refund from RRSP contribution to pay down the RRSP loan immediately

───────────────────────────────────────
CAR / VEHICLE LOAN
───────────────────────────────────────
Secured by the vehicle
Competitive rates vs unsecured personal loans
Can be used for: New vehicles, used vehicles, vehicle repairs
Terms and payment amounts depend on vehicle value, credit score, down payment

───────────────────────────────────────
4-H LOAN (Agricultural Youth)
───────────────────────────────────────
For 4-H members to purchase livestock or finance approved 4-H projects
ATB is proud to support Alberta's agricultural community and youth programs

COMMON CUSTOMER QUESTIONS — BORROWING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q: What is a good credit score to get a loan at ATB?
A: ATB doesn't publish a specific minimum credit score, but a higher score leads to better rates and higher approved amounts. Clients can check their credit score through ATB Personal if they are existing borrowing clients.

Q: Can I pay off my personal loan early?
A: Yes. ATB loans allow skip-a-payment (1 per year) and early repayment. Check your loan agreement for any prepayment restrictions — personal loans are generally more flexible than mortgages.

Q: How is my line of credit different from a credit card?
A: A LOC typically has a lower interest rate than a credit card. The minimum payment on a LOC is interest-only on the balance; a credit card minimum includes interest plus a portion of the principal balance. Both are revolving. A LOC is better for larger purchases over time; a credit card is more convenient for everyday spending and may offer rewards.

Q: Can I consolidate my credit card debt with an ATB loan?
A: Yes. An ATB debt consolidation loan pays off your high-interest credit card balances, and you repay ATB at a lower interest rate in a structured payment plan.

Q: What is the interest rate on a personal line of credit?
A: Variable, based on ATB prime rate plus a spread. The specific rate depends on your credit profile. ATB Advantage Account holders receive a discount of up to 2.5% on approved LOC rates (minimum Prime + 3%). Confirm the current rate with an ATB advisor or at atb.com/resources/rates.

Q: Can a student get a line of credit without a credit history?
A: Yes. The Students First LOC is available to students with good or unestablished credit. The Credit Builder LOC ($10,000) is available without credit history for newcomers to credit.

Q: What is the maximum I can borrow with a Students First LOC?
A: Up to $60,000 total (up to $15,000 per academic year). Repayment begins 12 months after graduation.

Q: I'm a medical student. Is there a higher limit available?
A: Yes. The Professional Students LOC is available for medical and dentistry students with up to $350,000 in financing at a preferred rate, with up to 25 years to repay. Post-graduation, up to $50,000 can be retained as a revolving Personal LOC.
